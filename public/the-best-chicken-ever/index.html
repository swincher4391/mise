The BEST Crispy Honey Garlic Chicken (Family Favorite!)

There are certain recipes that just feel like home. You know the ones I'm talking about ‚Äî the meals that transport you back to a specific moment in time, a specific kitchen, a specific feeling of warmth and safety. For me, this crispy honey garlic chicken is one of those recipes. Every time I make it, I'm instantly transported back to my grandmother's kitchen in rural Vermont, where the windows would fog up from whatever was simmering on the stove and the whole house smelled like garlic and love.

My grandmother ‚Äî we all called her Nana Mae ‚Äî was the kind of cook who never measured anything. She'd just toss things into a pan with this quiet confidence that I've spent my entire adult life trying to channel. She passed away when I was seventeen, and I'll never forget the last meal she made me: chicken thighs with a sticky, sweet glaze that she said was "just a little bit of this and a little bit of that." I've spent years trying to recreate it. This recipe is the closest I've ever come.

How a Trip to Thailand Changed Everything

A few years ago, my husband Josh and I took a trip to Chiang Mai, Thailand. It was supposed to be a "quick getaway" before our third baby arrived (spoiler: there is no such thing as a quick getaway when you're seven months pregnant and craving pad see ew at 2am). We took a cooking class at this tiny little place on the outskirts of the old city, where a woman named Khun Lek taught us how to balance sweet, salty, sour, and spicy in a single dish.

That class changed everything for me. I came home and immediately started rethinking every sauce I'd ever made. The honey garlic combination that Nana Mae used to make? It was already doing the sweet-salty thing. All it needed was a little acid and a little heat. Rice vinegar and sriracha. That was it. That was the missing piece I'd been looking for all those years.

I remember standing in my kitchen at 11pm, eight months pregnant, tears streaming down my face because I had finally nailed it. Josh came downstairs and found me eating chicken thighs straight out of the skillet. "Is everything okay?" he asked. "Nana Mae would be so proud," I told him. He wisely decided not to question the crying pregnant woman eating chicken over the stove and just asked if there was any left for him.

Why This Recipe Works (A Love Letter to Chicken Thighs)

I need to talk about chicken thighs for a minute. I know, I know ‚Äî chicken breast is the "healthy" choice. I spent my entire twenties buying boneless skinless chicken breasts and wondering why my dinners tasted like cardboard wrapped in disappointment. Then I discovered thighs and I have never looked back. They're more forgiving, more flavorful, and they actually stay juicy even if you overcook them by a minute or two (which, let's be honest, happens to all of us when we're also helping with homework, refereeing a fight about whose turn it is on the iPad, and trying to remember if we switched the laundry).

The key to this recipe is getting that skin CRISPY. I'm talking shatteringly, addictively, you'll-eat-the-skin-before-it-makes-it-to-the-plate crispy. The secret? Pat those thighs dry. I mean really dry. Use paper towels, do it twice, do it three times. Moisture is the enemy of crispy skin. Then you put them skin-side down in a hot pan and you DO NOT TOUCH THEM. I know it's hard. I know you want to peek. Do not peek. Walk away. Set a timer. Go fold that laundry you forgot about. Come back in seven minutes and prepare to be amazed.

My son Ethan (four) ate three pieces and then asked if chickens have feelings. (We had a whole conversation about that. Dinner got philosophical.) Baby Charlotte just smashed hers into her hair, which I'm choosing to interpret as enthusiasm.

Josh has officially declared this his "death row meal," which is morbid but also very flattering. His coworkers have started requesting it when they come over for football Sundays. His friend Dave, who claims to not like "Asian-inspired food" (Dave is wrong about many things), ate five pieces and asked for the recipe. I told him it was on my blog. He said he doesn't read blogs. So if you're reading this, Dave, I told you so.

Tips for Making This Recipe Perfect

I've made this recipe probably 200 times at this point (not exaggerating ‚Äî we eat it at least once a week, sometimes twice if I'm being honest). Here are my best tips after years of tweaking:

First, the honey. Please use good honey. I know it's tempting to grab that bear-shaped bottle at the grocery store, and honestly? It'll still be delicious. But if you can find a local raw honey, the depth of flavor is just incredible. My friend Margaret keeps bees in her backyard (she's that kind of person and I love her for it), and when I use her honey in this recipe, it's transcendent.

Second, don't skip the cornstarch slurry. I know it seems like an extra step, but it's what takes the sauce from "thin liquid pooling sadly on your plate" to "thick, glossy, cling-to-every-inch-of-the-chicken glaze." It takes thirty seconds. Do it.

Third, and this is important ‚Äî let the chicken rest for just a minute or two after you coat it in the sauce. I know you're hungry. I know it smells incredible. But that brief rest lets the sauce set up and get even stickier. Trust me on this one.

Crispy Honey Garlic Chicken

Crispy pan-seared chicken thighs glazed with a sticky honey garlic sauce. Ready in 30 minutes!

Prep: 10 min Cook: 20 min Total: 30 min Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 8 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1/3 cup honey
  • 1/4 cup soy sauce
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 1 tablespoon sriracha
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch mixed with 2 tablespoons water
  • 2 green onions, sliced
  • 1 tablespoon sesame seeds

Instructions

  1. Pat chicken thighs dry with paper towels. Season generously on both sides with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  2. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Place chicken skin-side down and cook for 7-8 minutes until the skin is deeply golden and crispy. Flip and cook 6-7 minutes more until cooked through (165°F internal). Remove to a plate.
  3. Drain all but 1 tablespoon of fat from the skillet. Reduce heat to medium and add the minced garlic. Sauté for 30 seconds until fragrant.
  4. Add honey, soy sauce, rice vinegar, sriracha, and sesame oil to the skillet. Stir to combine and bring to a simmer.
  5. Pour in the cornstarch slurry and stir constantly for 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens and becomes glossy.
  6. Return the chicken to the skillet and spoon the sauce over each piece, turning to coat. Let it simmer together for 1 minute. Serve garnished with sliced green onions and sesame seeds.
Sarah's Notes: Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. Reheat in a skillet over medium heat to re-crisp the skin. To make it spicier, double the sriracha. For a milder version, omit the sriracha and add a squeeze of fresh lime juice instead.
